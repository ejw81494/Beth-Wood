How My Thanksgiving Break Has Been

I work as a Patient Care Assistant for individuals with Dementia. This is a job that I really value but let me tell you, it is hard work. I worked on Thanksgiving, Black Friday, and two twelve hour shifts the following two days. Combined with the fact that it is the end of the school semester, I feel horribly exhausted. If something happens to me, know that I had a heart attack from the stress. My family, who lives in Forsyth County, got together a day early to celebrate Thanksgiving so that I could be there. I was very glad to see everyone. I made the "dressing", which I have never done and actually isn't a dish that I typically like, but it was very good and my little sister's boyfriend said it was actually the best he had ever had.

My College History

I started college at GCSU in Milledgeville, studying Environmental Science. I got into UGA despite the fact that I was a pretty crappy high school student. Believe it or not, I actually was offered a little scholarship money as well. I don't remember that because I was adamant about not attending UGA regardless of acceptance, because I thought a small town would be so cute. I was wrong, it was lonely, and now I am here without the scholarship money. Go figure. I briefly entertained a degree in Geology, a subject I love, and then went crazy and had a hardship withdrawal. I think I did this several times, because I also tried to pursue Biology, which was horrible. I have learned that even if things are going horribly, just finish it out, even in flames. I am majoring now in Anthropology, which is what I enjoy most and what is easy, but I am also majoring in Ag Econ, which is interesting but frankly I don't know if it is the right direction for me, my dad just pushes me in a "practical" direction. I may end up wanting to simply major in Anthropology and become a nurse. I am good at caring for others.
